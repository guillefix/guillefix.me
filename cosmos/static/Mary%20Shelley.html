Mary Shelley

cosmos 12th December 2018 at 12:30am
Author

Author of Frankenkstein (Frankenstein; or, The Modern Prometheus), a XIX Gothic novel, which shook England and Europe for its daring and direct confrontation of topics such as the creation of life by man, materialism, the origin of the soul (Vitalism debate), the effect of Science on life; its beauty and promise, but also its Terror, otherworldiness; the tension between romanticism and science (does science desensitize people, or does it heighten the sense, as some in the Age of Wonder would have argued, including poets and scientists).

Many of the ideas were inspired by "galvanic" experiments and demonstrations, including demonstration to bring people and animals to life again, some performed by an italian scientist on the streets of London. She was probably inspired by some work by German scientists (see Age of Wonder book; chapter Frankenstein and the soul for a wonderful account)

She also edited and promoted the works of her husband, the Romantic poet and philosopher Percy Bysshe Shelley, who was also friends with Humphry Davy
