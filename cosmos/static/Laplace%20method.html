Laplace method

1. Restrict integral to a small region (of order ϵ\epsilonϵ) around maxima of exponential function ϕ\phiϕ, and confirm we are making an exponentially small error.
2. Expand f(t)f(t)f(t) and ϕ(t)\phi(t)ϕ(t) in series valid in this region, so we get a series of integrals.
3. It is then usually easier to evaluate these integrals by extending the limits to infinity (after rescaling), confirming that we are again making an exponentially small error.
4. Confirm assumptions are self-consistent.
