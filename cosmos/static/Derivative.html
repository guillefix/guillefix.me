The derivative of a function f:X→Rf: X \to \mathbb{R}f:X→R for XXX a Differentiable manifold is defined as an element of the Cotangent space, which in coordinates corresponds to the Gradient of the function. In the case of XXX being Rn\mathbb{R}^nR​n​​, this corresponds to the usual derivative in Multivariable calculus.

Other derivative-like notions in Differential geometry

Differential (Jacobian, for maps between manifolds)

Vector fields. Derivative of a function (to R\mathbb{R}R) on a manifold, along a flow on the manifold, resulting on a new function on the manifold (to R\mathbb{R}R). This is basically a field of rank-1 differentials (Jacobians which are just vectors).
