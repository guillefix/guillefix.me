Category theory

Informally, it is a general way of describing algebraic structures.

A monad is a Category C\mathcal{C}C, together with a Functor T:C→CT: \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}T:C→C from that category to itself, equipped with two Natural transformations η:1C⇒T \eta: 1_\mathcal{C} \Rightarrow Tη:1​C​​⇒T and μ:T2⇒T\mu: T^2 \Rightarrow Tμ:T​2​​⇒T satsifying unit and associativity axioms.

Every adjunction gives rise to a monad (Adjunction) and every monad gives an adjunction between the underlying set of the monad, and the category of algebras over that monad. Also every monad gives an adjunction to the Kleisli category.
