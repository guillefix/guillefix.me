The Church as a power. A democratic organization where intellectuals could work (although with increasing conflicts with politics and faith). It was a structure that help in keeping civilization alive during the middle ages, and provided the spirit of the times, spiritually and in action: the building of the churches and cathedrals, pilgrimages. Where some way of thought or human activity is really vital to us, then internationalism is accepted unhesitatingly (then, in the church, now in Science).

However, as tends to happen with big powers, those who threaten the power, are in danger. People who promoted doubt and questioning like the eminent Peter Abelard, who in 1122, said "The beginning of wisdom is found in doubting; by doubting we come to questioning, and by questioning we perceive the truth."

First great church, where the monastic tradition began was the Abbey of Cluney, the biggest church at the time (but of which most has been lost, including its magnificient ornaments)
