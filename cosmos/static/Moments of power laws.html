Moments of power laws

guillefix 4th November 2016 at 1:43pm

As can be shown again by approximating sum by integral, all the moments ⟨km⟩\langle k^m \rangle⟨k​m​​⟩of a power law distribution diverge for m>α−1m>\alpha-1m>α−1. Of course, this is in the limit of an infinite system with the same distribution, in finite systems (as in networks with a finite number of nodes), the moment will of course be finite (for a network, kkk will have a maximum value, cutting off the domain of the integral used to calculate CCC).
