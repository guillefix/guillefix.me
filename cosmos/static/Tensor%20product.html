We can also define an operation ⊗:V×W→V⊗W\otimes: V\times W \to V \otimes W⊗:V×W→V⊗W, which takes v∈Vv \in Vv∈V and w∈Ww\in Ww∈W to v⊗w∈V⊗Wv \otimes w \in V \otimes Wv⊗w∈V⊗W, and which is bilinear.

In terms of Category theory, it can be defined as being the vector space V⊗WV\otimes WV⊗W which has a universal property that if B:V×W→UB: V \times W \to UB:V×W→U is a bilinear map to a vector space UUU then there is a unique linear map β:V⊗W→U\beta: V \otimes W \to Uβ:V⊗W→U such that B(v,w)=β(v⊗w)B(v,w) = \beta(v \otimes w)B(v,w)=β(v⊗w).

We can also define V⊗WV \otimes WV⊗W as the dual space of the space of bilinear forms on V×WV \times WV×W.
