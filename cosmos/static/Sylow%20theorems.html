First Sylow theorem

Gives partial converse to Lagrange's theorem

For ppp a Prime number

∣G∣=pmr|G| = p^m r∣G∣=p​m​​r, where ppp does not divide rrr.

Then there is always a Subgroup with pmp^mp​m​​ elements. Such subgroup is called Sylow p-group.

Second Sylow theorem

In particular it says, all Sylow subgroups are Conjugated to each other.

Let subgroup K⊂GK \subset GK⊂G, whose order is divisible by ppp. Let HHH be a Sylow p-subgroup of GGG. Then there is a conjugate subgroup H′=gHg−1H' = gHg^{-1}H​′​​=gHg​−1​​ of GGG, such that K∩H′K \cap H'K∩H​′​​ is a p-Sylow subgroup of KKK.

Corollaries.

  1. Let K⊂GK \subset GK⊂G be a subgroup which is a p-group. Then KKK is contained in a p-Sylow subgroup of GGG.
  2. The Sylow p-subgroups of GGG are all conjugate

As conjugation is bijection, the conjugate of a Sylow p-subgroup is a Sylow p-subgroup.
