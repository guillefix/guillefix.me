Integral domain

cosmos 21st October 2017 at 11:56pm
Commutative ring

Def. A unital Commutative ring without Zero divisors is called an integral domain

Remark (Cancelation property). In an integral domain, we can cancel factors in products, like in Groups.

Z6\mathbb{Z}_6Z​6​​ (integers modulo 666) is not an integral domain.

If ppp is a Prime number, Zp\mathbb{Z}_pZ​p​​ is an integral domain.
