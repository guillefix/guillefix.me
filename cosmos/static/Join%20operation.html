A join, ∨\vee∨ is an operation defined on elements of a poset PPP (not necessarily all of them) defined as:

The join (or Least upper bound) of a,b∈Pa, b \in Pa,b∈P is an element a∨b∈pa \vee b \in pa∨b∈p such that:

(a) a∨ba \vee ba∨b is an upper bound of aaa and bbb: thus a⪯a∨ba \preceq a \vee ba⪯a∨b and b⪯a∨bb \preceq a \vee bb⪯a∨b;
(b) a∨ba \vee ba∨b is the least such upper bound: i.e., if there exists c∈Pc \in Pc∈P such that a⪯ca \preceq ca⪯c and b⪯cb \preceq cb⪯c then a∨b⪯ca \vee b \preceq ca∨b⪯c.

Note that, if it exists, a join is necessarily unique.
